                          JUDGMENT
The first petitioner had purchased a goods vehicle and

submitted an application for converting the same into a

Generator Van.       The first petitioner wanted to fix a

generator set therein, for being used as part of Cinema

Out door Units.       The vehicle has subsequently been

purchased by the second petitioner. The alteration of the

vehicle was not permitted on the ground that the same

was against the provisions of
Section 52
of the Motor

Vehicles Act.
2. I have disposed of other Writ Petitions where

similar issues had arisen for determination as per my

judgments    in    W.P.(c)      No.2738/2010          and W.P.(C)

No.3108/2010. As per the said judgments, I have found

that alteration of a passenger vehicle by fitting a

generator set does not amount to alteration of the basic

feature of the vehicle since no alteration is made to the
wpc No.7032/2010            2
chassis. The vehicle in the present case is a goods vehicle.

   Therefore, the dictum in the said case applies with

   stronger force in this case also because there is not even a

   conversion of the vehicle from a passenger vehicle to

   goods vehicle. Therefore, this Writ Petition is only to be

   allowed.
3. Accordingly, Ext.P1 order is set aside.        The

   respondent shall grant permission for alteration of the

   goods vehicle bearing Reg.No.KL-7/F 6123 as a generator

   van as sought for by the petitioner within a period of three

   weeks from the date of receipt of a copy of this judgment.
