                               JUDGMENT
The petitioner challenges the initiation of disciplinary

proceedings by the administrator in office.                    The contention

raised is that the power to dismiss has to be treated as available

only to a person or authority with a power to appoint and

therefore,   the   administrator           cannot        initiate disciplinary

proceedings which may visit the petitioner with the penalty of

dismissal from service. That question need not be answered in

this case for the very simple reason that the election to the

committee of the society is scheduled to be held on 7.9.2008.

The power of the administrator to initiate disciplinary

proceedings cannot be disputed.                If at all, all that could be

disputed is the power to impose a particular punishment.

Therefore, the initiation of enquiry proceedings and the power of

the enquiry officer cannot be found fault with. Hence, this writ

petition is ordered directing that though enquiry proceedings

could be completed by the enquiry officer, 4th respondent,

enquiry officer will consider any application by the petitioner for

leave to appear and continue to participate in those proceedings.

All other contentions are left open.
