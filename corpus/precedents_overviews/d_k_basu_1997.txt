CASE: D.K. Basu v State of West Bengal (1997)

KEY FACTS: Acting on letters highlighting frequent deaths in police lock-ups and custody, the Court treated the correspondence as a public interest writ petition and examined custodial violence as an all-India problem.

PRINCIPLE: Custodial torture and death violate the right to life and dignity under Article 21 and the protection against self-incrimination. To safeguard arrestees, the Court laid down binding requirements for every arrest and detention — including an arrest memo attested by a witness and countersigned by the arrestee, informing a relative or friend, a medical examination, and recording details in a register. Monetary compensation is payable by the State for established custodial violations.

HOLDING: Eleven mandatory arrest/detention guidelines were issued, binding on all agencies until suitable legislation, with departmental and contempt liability for breach.

COURT: Supreme Court of India
AREA: constitutional law — custodial rights, Article 21
