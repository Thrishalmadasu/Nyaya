CASE: K.S. Puttaswamy v Union of India — Privacy (2017)

KEY FACTS: Challenges to the Aadhaar biometric identity scheme raised the threshold question whether the Constitution guarantees a fundamental right to privacy, given earlier observations to the contrary. A nine-judge bench was constituted to settle the issue.

PRINCIPLE: The right to privacy is a fundamental right, intrinsic to the right to life and personal liberty under Article 21 and to the freedoms guaranteed by Part III. Privacy includes the preservation of personal intimacies, bodily autonomy, and informational self-determination. Any intrusion must satisfy the tests of legality, a legitimate State aim, and proportionality. The contrary view in M.P. Sharma and Kharak Singh was overruled to that extent.

HOLDING: The Court unanimously declared that privacy is a constitutionally protected fundamental right.

COURT: Supreme Court of India (9-judge bench)
AREA: constitutional law — right to privacy, Article 21
