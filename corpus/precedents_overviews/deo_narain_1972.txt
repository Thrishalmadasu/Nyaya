CASE: Deo Narain v State of UP (1972)

KEY FACTS: In a clash over possession of land, the appellant inflicted a fatal spear blow to the chest of a member of the opposing party, having himself received only a superficial lathi blow. The High Court convicted him under Section 304 IPC, holding that he had exceeded the right of private defence by using greater force than necessary.

PRINCIPLE: The right of private defence of the body arises as soon as a reasonable apprehension of danger to the body comes into existence from an attempt or threat to commit an offence; the defender need not wait until he actually sustains a serious injury. The reasonableness of the apprehension — not the injury in fact suffered — governs the permissible force. It is a preventive, not punitive, right.

HOLDING: The Supreme Court allowed the appeal, holding the High Court erred; the accused was entitled to the right of private defence.

COURT: Supreme Court of India
AREA: criminal law — right of private defence (Sections 100, 102 IPC)
