CASE: Gudikanti Narasimhulu v Public Prosecutor (1978)

KEY FACTS: Convicts whose acquittal was under challenge in a State appeal, and who had surrendered to custody before preferring a statutory appeal, applied for bail pending the hearing of that appeal.

PRINCIPLE: The "bail or jail" decision, at the pre-trial or post-conviction stage, turns on judicial discretion structured by relevant considerations rather than caprice. Personal liberty under Article 21 is fundamental and may be curtailed only by a procedure that is reasonable and even-handed. Relevant factors include the nature and gravity of the charge, the severity of likely punishment, the risk of the accused absconding or evading justice, the danger of tampering with witnesses or evidence, and the accused's antecedents. Refusal of bail is justified for securing justice, not as punishment.

HOLDING: Bail was granted; the judgment articulated the guiding jurisprudence of bail.

COURT: Supreme Court of India
AREA: criminal procedure — bail, personal liberty (Article 21)
