CASE: Olga Tellis v Bombay Municipal Corporation (1985)

KEY FACTS: Pavement and slum dwellers in Bombay faced forcible eviction and demolition of their hutments by the Municipal Corporation under the Bombay Municipal Corporation Act. They contended that eviction would destroy their means of livelihood and therefore their right to life.

PRINCIPLE: The right to life under Article 21 includes the right to livelihood, since no person can live without the means of living. Depriving pavement dwellers of their livelihood by eviction implicates Article 21 and can only be done by a just and fair procedure. There can be no estoppel against the enforcement of fundamental rights. The Corporation's power to remove encroachments is valid, but must be exercised reasonably and after hearing.

HOLDING: Evictions must follow fair procedure (notice and opportunity to be heard); subject to that, the Corporation could remove encroachments on public footpaths.

COURT: Supreme Court of India
AREA: constitutional law — right to livelihood, Article 21
