CASE: State of Punjab v Gurmit Singh (1996)

KEY FACTS: A schoolgirl under sixteen was abducted in a car while going home after an examination and raped by the accused. The trial court acquitted them, disbelieving the prosecutrix over minor discrepancies and casting aspersions on her character.

PRINCIPLE: The testimony of a victim of sexual assault stands on a footing similar to that of an injured witness and, if it inspires confidence, can by itself sustain a conviction without corroboration. Courts must not disbelieve a prosecutrix for minor contradictions or insignificant discrepancies, must avoid casting unjustified stigma on her character, and should conduct such trials with sensitivity, ordinarily in camera, to protect the victim's dignity.

HOLDING: The Supreme Court set aside the acquittal and convicted the accused, censuring the trial court's insensitive approach.

COURT: Supreme Court of India
AREA: criminal law — rape, evidentiary value of the prosecutrix's testimony
