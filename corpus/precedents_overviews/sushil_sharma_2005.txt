CASE: Sushil Kumar Sharma v Union of India (2005)

KEY FACTS: A public interest petition sought a declaration that Section 498-A IPC (cruelty to a married woman by husband or his relatives) is unconstitutional, or alternatively guidelines to prevent its misuse, citing instances of false and frivolous complaints used to harass husbands and their relatives.

PRINCIPLE: Section 498-A IPC is constitutionally valid; the object of combating dowry-related cruelty is legitimate. The mere possibility or fact of abuse of a provision by some litigants does not render the provision itself unconstitutional — the remedy lies in guarding against misuse, not in striking down the law. Courts must be vigilant to ensure that the provision is not used as a weapon for personal vendetta against innocent persons.

HOLDING: The prayer to declare Section 498-A unconstitutional was rejected; the Court cautioned against misuse and left it to the legislature/courts to curb abuse.

COURT: Supreme Court of India
AREA: constitutional and criminal law — validity of Section 498-A IPC
