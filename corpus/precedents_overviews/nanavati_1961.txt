CASE: K.M. Nanavati v State of Maharashtra (1961)

KEY FACTS: A naval officer, on learning that his wife had an affair with Ahuja, went to his ship, obtained a revolver and cartridges on a pretext, drove to Ahuja's flat, and shot him dead. He claimed grave and sudden provocation and that the shots went off accidentally during a struggle.

PRINCIPLE: To reduce murder to culpable homicide under Exception 1 to Section 300 IPC, the provocation must be both grave and sudden, and the accused must act while deprived of self-control before there is time for passion to cool. A time interval allowing the accused to plan and arm himself shows the act was deliberate, not the product of sustained loss of self-control. The case also clarified the High Court's powers on a reference where the jury's verdict is perverse.

HOLDING: The plea of grave and sudden provocation failed; the conviction for murder under Section 302 IPC was upheld.

COURT: Supreme Court of India
AREA: criminal law — murder, grave and sudden provocation
