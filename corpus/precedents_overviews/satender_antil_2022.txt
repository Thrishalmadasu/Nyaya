CASE: Satender Kumar Antil v CBI (2022)

KEY FACTS: Prompted by a continuous flow of bail applications arising from a wrong interpretation of Section 170 CrPC and routine arrests after the filing of the chargesheet, the Court, assisted by senior counsel, undertook to lay down bail guidelines categorised by type of offence.

PRINCIPLE: Bail is the rule and jail the exception. Offences were grouped into categories (A–D, by punishment and special statutes) with calibrated guidance for granting bail. The Court emphasised compliance with Sections 41 and 41-A CrPC (per Arnesh Kumar), the limited need for arrest where the accused cooperated during investigation, and timely disposal of bail pleas; it urged the Government to consider a dedicated Bail Act.

HOLDING: Comprehensive, category-wise bail guidelines were issued to the courts below, while preserving judicial discretion on the facts of each case.

COURT: Supreme Court of India
AREA: criminal procedure — bail, arrest (Sections 41, 41-A, 170 CrPC)
