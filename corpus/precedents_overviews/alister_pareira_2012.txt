CASE: Alister Anthony Pareira v State of Maharashtra (2012)

KEY FACTS: In the early hours of 12 November 2006 at Carter Road, Bandra (West), Mumbai, a car driven by the intoxicated appellant ran onto a pavement, killing seven persons and injuring eight who were sleeping there.

PRINCIPLE: A person who drives a vehicle rashly and while heavily intoxicated, with knowledge that his act is likely to cause death, may be convicted of culpable homicide not amounting to murder under Section 304 Part II IPC — not merely of the lesser offence of causing death by rash or negligent act under Section 304A. Knowledge of likely fatal consequences supplies the mens rea for Section 304 Part II.

HOLDING: The conviction under Section 304 Part II IPC (with related offences) was upheld and the sentence enhanced.

COURT: Supreme Court of India
AREA: criminal law — drunken driving, culpable homicide (Section 304 Part II IPC)
