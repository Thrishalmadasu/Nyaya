CASE: Hussainara Khatoon v Home Secretary, State of Bihar (1979)

KEY FACTS: A habeas corpus petition revealed that a large number of undertrial prisoners — including women and children — had been languishing in Bihar jails for years awaiting trial, many for periods far exceeding the maximum sentence their alleged offences could attract.

PRINCIPLE: The right to a speedy trial is a fundamental right implicit in the right to life and personal liberty under Article 21. A procedure that keeps people in detention without trial for long periods cannot be regarded as "reasonable, just or fair", and so fails the Article 21 standard. The right to free legal aid to an accused who cannot afford a lawyer is also part of just procedure.

HOLDING: The Court directed the release of the undertrial prisoners on personal bonds without monetary sureties.

COURT: Supreme Court of India
AREA: constitutional law — speedy trial, free legal aid, Article 21
